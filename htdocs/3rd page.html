Let's Define These Terms

/mis•in•for•ma•tion/

noun

false information that is spread, regardless of intent to mislead

/dis•in•for•ma•tion/

noun

/deliberately misleading or biased information; manipulated narrative or facts; propaganda/

/in•fo•de•mic/

noun

a blend of "information" and "epidemic" that typically refers to a rapid and far-reaching spread of both accurate and inaccurate information about something, such as a disease

/fake•news/

noun

false or misleading information presented as news

news that misinforms or disinforms the public about important topics

Why have these terminologies popped up recently?

These terms, such as infodemic, have been used more ever since the COVID-19 pandemic has struck, even though the world has already had issues with fake news before. The dangers of misinformation and disinformation were made more evident during this pandemic, with the public being misinformed about safety protocols and even the validity of vaccines. These troubling times are already hard on Filipinos, but misinformation and disinformation have made the crowd even more confused about what is happening.

Why is fake news so efficient in misinforming the public?

Fake news often feeds off people's curiosity and targets people more susceptible to being misinformed and people who tend to share with other people without fact-checking sources. Fake news only becomes effective if people reading believe in it. That is why checking sources is a must nowadays, especially when fake news and misinformation have plagued the internet world.
